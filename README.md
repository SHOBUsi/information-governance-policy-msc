# Information Governance Policy — Cafe de Fantastica

A professional Information Governance Policy designed for *Cafe de Fantastica Ltd*, a hypothetical UK-based café chain. This project demonstrates how a real-world organisation should manage, protect, and govern its information assets in line with UK law and international security standards.

---

## What Is This?

This is a complete **Information Governance Policy (IGP)**, a formal document that defines how an organisation should handle its data. It covers everything from who is responsible for data to how long records are kept, to what happens when something goes wrong.

It was built for a hypothetical café business, but the policy itself is designed to be realistic and fit-for-purpose as if it were being handed to a real organisation to implement.

---

## Why Was It Built?

*Cafe de Fantastica* is a tech-dependent business. It handles customer orders online, stores employee and payment data, works with third-party delivery partners, and runs cloud-based systems. All of that creates real data risk.

Without a governance policy, the organisation would have:
- No clear ownership of data
- No plan when a breach happens
- No way to prove legal compliance
- No structure for keeping or deleting records safely

This policy solves all of that.

---

## What Does It Cover?

**Who is responsible** — Clear roles defined for the Board, CIO, CRO, IT Manager, HR Manager, Branch Managers, and all staff.

**Legal compliance** — Built around UK GDPR, Data Protection Act 2018, PECR, PCI-DSS, and the Computer Misuse Act 1990.

**International standards** — Aligned with ISO/IEC 27001 (information security), ISO 22301 (business continuity), ISO 31000 (risk management), and NIST SP 800-30 (risk assessment).

**Data lifecycle** — How data is classified, accessed, stored, retained, and securely disposed of.

**Risk & recovery** — A risk management framework and a disaster recovery plan with defined Recovery Time and Recovery Point Objectives (RTO/RPO).

**Ethics** — Data minimisation, fairness, transparency, and accountability are built into the governance approach.

**Implementation** — A five-stage rollout strategy covering planning, infrastructure, training, monitoring, and continuous improvement.

---

## How Was It Made?

The policy was researched and written from the ground up, drawing on:
- UK regulatory guidance (ICO, HMRC)
- International security and governance standards
- Academic literature on information governance and cybersecurity
- Real-world best practices for data classification, access control, and incident response

The accompanying report critically analyses every decision made in the policy justifying the legal basis, the chosen frameworks, and the implementation approach.
